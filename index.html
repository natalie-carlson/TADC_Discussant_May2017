‘Entrepreneuring’ solutions to social problems

Nevena Radoynovska, Northwestern Kellogg

Discussant: Natalie Carlson, Columbia Business School / ncarlson19@gsb.columbia.edu

Unique aspects of this paper

  • Focus on myths and illusions, rather than more traditional sucess outcomes
  • Thorough grounding in French context
  • Diverse set of players in two (mostly disjoint) areas of social entrepreneurship

Strengths

  • Clarity of writing and the structured nature of the findings
  • Inclusion of closed or disbanded organizations in sample to account for survivor bias

Elements to clarify

  • More details on "supporting organizations" (SOs)
  • How do the myths you identify interact with the different subject pools?

Well done!
